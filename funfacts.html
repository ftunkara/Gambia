The beautiful landscape of The Gambia

  1. Smallest Country on Mainland Africa
    The Gambia is the smallest country on mainland Africa, stretching about 480 km (300 miles) along the Gambia River and only about 50 km (30 miles) wide at its broadest point.
  2. Entire Country Is Built Around One River
    The Gambia is literally wrapped around the Gambia River, which flows from east to west and is central to life, trade, farming, and culture.
  3. Surrounded by Just One Country
    It's almost completely surrounded by Senegal, except for its short Atlantic coastline.
  4. Known as "The Smiling Coast of Africa"
    Gambians are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness, giving the country this nickname loved by locals and tourists alike.
  5. English Is the Official Language
    Though many local languages like Mandinka, Wolof, Fula, and Jola are spoken, English is the official language due to Gambia's British colonial history.
  6. Home to Over 500 Bird Species üê¶
    Bird watchers love The Gambia! It's one of the best places in West Africa for birdwatching, with vibrant species like bee-eaters, hornbills, and kingfishers.
  7. Roots of Kunta Kinteh
    The famous character Kunta Kinteh, from Alex Haley's Roots, was based on a real Gambian from the village of Juffureh. Today, Kunta Kinteh Island (formerly James Island) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
